Allstate is "committed to helping survivors build their financial skills as a way to escape abusive relationships,&nbsp;get safe, stay safe and&nbsp;thrive."</p> <p style="padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 12px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px;">Our Case Manager and Counselor have been trained in the Allstate's <em>Moving Ahead Through Financial Management</em> curriculum and are using its modules in group and individual sessions. &nbsp;Clients have an opportunity to save up to $1000 and have those funds matched from the Allstate Foundation grant dollars. &nbsp;The Foundation notes that they are "committed to using financial empowerment strategies to end the cycle of violence because research shows that financial dependency is the strongest predictor of a survivor's decision to remain, leave or return to an abusive relationship - even stronger than physical safety issues." &nbsp;We are excited to see the difference that this opportunity makes in our clients' lives.</p> <p style="padding-top: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-bottom: 12px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px;"><img src="images/all_fnd_grad_ver_4pro_pos.jpg" alt="" width="400" height="400" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" /></p></div> <div class="feed-description"><h1>Allstate Foundation Grant Funds Awarded to MSP</h1> <p>Through a grant application with the Ohio Domestic Violence Network, My Sister's Place has been awarded funding from the Allstate Foundation to bring&nbsp;financial education, a matched savings program, job readiness and job training and microenterprise opportunities to survivors of domestic violence.
