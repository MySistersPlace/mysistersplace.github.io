Allstate Foundation Grant Funds Awarded to MSP

Through a grant application with the Ohio Domestic Violence Network, My Sister's Place has been awarded funding from the Allstate Foundation to bring financial education, a matched savings program, job readiness and job training and microenterprise opportunities to survivors of domestic violence. Allstate is "committed to helping survivors build their financial skills as a way to escape abusive relationships, get safe, stay safe and thrive."

Our Case Manager and Counselor have been trained in the Allstate's Moving Ahead Through Financial Management curriculum and are using its modules in group and individual sessions.  Clients have an opportunity to save up to $1000 and have those funds matched from the Allstate Foundation grant dollars.  The Foundation notes that they are "committed to using financial empowerment strategies to end the cycle of violence because research shows that financial dependency is the strongest predictor of a survivor's decision to remain, leave or return to an abusive relationship - even stronger than physical safety issues."  We are excited to see the difference that this opportunity makes in our clients' lives.
